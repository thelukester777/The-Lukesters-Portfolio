Hello, I'm Luke—a working class web developer in the great city of Chicago, Illinois. Feel free to click around the navigation to explore the site!

A little about me: I studied 3D Modeling, Animation, Illustration, Digital Design, and more at 'The American Academy of Art.' It was there I honed my artisitc ability and wet my apetite for the commercial arts. After graduating, I cut my teeth as a developer at a small photography company. I've been at it ever since. Today, I hold a great deal of industry experience after working in ecommerce for over decade.

Sears Holdings Corporation

At SHC, I started out as a Junior Web Developer, worked my way up to Senior Web Developer, and then finally became a Web Development Manager in 2014. During this time period, I worked with many different people on various teams throughout the organization to develop everything from simple web banners, to full-fledged web applications.

We utilized lots of software and web dev technology to fullfill the needs of project stakeholders. It was a fast-paced, deadline-driven environment, with lots of creative and passionate people from all walks of life.

Jun 2010 – Apr 2013

Just starting out at the company, my team was responsible for hard-coding content on the Sears and Kmart websites. We worked closely with the online-marketing team, as well as web designers to create promotional banners, landing pages, and social media campaigns. I was not only able to hone my design capabilities by developing templates for email campaigns, landing pages, banners, and other web content, I was also able to learn the basics of writing HTML and CSS. I worked closely with copywriters and back-end developers to design and currate content for the American, British, Dutch, and German versions of our website.

"Faces" - 2009

'Faces' is about not smiling. It represents the silly twisted faces people make when they aren't in the mood to pose for the camera. People in these moments typically reveal something you just don't get by telling them to "Say cheese!" I used the opportunity to play with light reflection and hunted for color combinations that compliment the mood of their expressions. While trying to keep them somewhat photo-realisitic—I amped up colors and warped features slightly to get a more animated look.

"The Alley Stores" - 2006-2007

While in college, I worked at The Alley stores, a popular punk-rock/ metal haven for buying alternative clothing and accessories. Over the course of a year, I designed over 50 t-shirt logos and even helped print a great deal of them. It was a very fun job and it was the first time I considered myself a commercial artist.

"Dare to Dream" - 2013

I got the idea for this after seeing a news bit about a turtle that had been tied to the end of a weather balloon. I was inspired to make this cruel story more light hearted. I decided that instead to portray a turtle who has always dreamed of flying, and now has reached his dream.

"Octopi Your Mind" - 2014

I was inspired by seeing a few drawings of octopi, and I thought it would be really fun to try creating my own version of one. I kept layering textures and playing with various colors until I got the array just right. It lacks a bit in technicality, but the colors are what makes this one special to me.

"Sea Floor Fight" - 2014

This was a fun idea I had shortly after re-touching the "Optopi Your Mind" piece. I was challeneged by a friend to start drawing again, so I took my sketchbook with me on my commutes for a week and came up with the first draft of this in pencil.

"Self Portrait" - 2006

This was one of the pieces in my senior gallery showing before graduating college. These days it looks a bit crude, but is still a very relevant piece to me, considering I learned a great deal about how to illustrate using digital media.
